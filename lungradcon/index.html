About LunGradCon

The 17th annual Lunar and Small Bodies Graduate Forum (LunGradCon 2026) will be held in-person at NASA Ames Research Center. The conference will take place from 9 AM to 4 PM in the 2nd floor conference room. Snacks and refreshments will be provided.

LunGradCon 2026 is intended to enhance the professional development of graduate students in lunar science by providing an opportunity to present and discuss scientific research in an environment of their peers. As such, the conference is both planned by and restricted to current undergraduate, graduate students and recent postdocs.

Following the broad interests of the Solar System Exploration Research Virtual Institute (SSERVI), the scope of LunGradCon includes both lunar and small body science. LunGradCon provides an opportunity for grad students and early-career postdocs to present their research on lunar and small body science in a low-stress, friendly environment, being critiqued only by their student and recent graduate peers. In addition to pre-recorded oral presentations, the forum presents opportunities for professional development and networking with fellow grad students and postdocs, as well as senior members of SSERVI.

LunGradCon will address the following research topics of the NASA Solar System Exploration Research Virtual Institute:

  • Dust/Regolith and Plasma
  • Geology and Geophysics
  • Volatiles/Exospheres
  • Missions and Human Exploration

Registration

If you are interested in attending or presenting at LunGradCon 2026, please fill out the registration form. Registration will be open until June 15th, 2026.

Conference Format

Abstracts are due to lungradcon@gmail.com by midnight on May 15th, 2026.

Talks will be 8 minutes in length.

After each speaker is done, the session chairs will open the floor to questions from the audience about any of the topics in the session, and general discussion will be encouraged.

The conference will also have poster session, grouped by the different topics delineated above. Please follow the NESF guidelines for poster size/format. More information will be provided regarding the format and schedule for poster presentations.

Abstracts

Abstracts must be emailed to lungradcon@gmail.com by May 15th, 11:59pm PDT. Decisions on abstracts will be given by May 30th. Please don't hesitate to reach out if you have issues meeting these deadlines.
