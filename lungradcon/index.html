About LunGradCon

The 14th annual Lunar and Small Bodies Graduate Forum (LunGradCon 2023) will be held in-person on July 17th at the Hotel at the University of Maryland, located at 7777 Baltimore Avenue, College Park, Maryland. For lodging, a group discount will be provided at the hotel with NESF.

LunGradCon 2022 intended to enhance the professional development of graduate students in lunar science by providing an opportunity to present and discuss scientific research in an environment of their peers. As such, the conference was both planned by and restricted to current graduate students.

Following the broad interests of the Solar System Exploration Research Virtual Institute (SSERVI), the scope of LunGradCon includes both lunar and small body science. LunGradCon provides an opportunity for grad students and early-career postdocs to present their research on lunar and small body science in a low-stress, friendly environment, being critiqued only by their student and recent graduate peers. In addition to pre-recorded oral presentations, the forum presents opportunities for professional development and networking with fellow grad students and postdocs, as well as senior members of SSERVI.

LunGradCon will address the following research topics of the NASA Solar System Exploration Research Virtual Institute:

  • Dust/Regolith and Plasma
  • Geology and Geophysics
  • Volatiles/Exospheres
  • Missions and Human Exploration

Registration

If you are interested in attending or presenting at LunGradCon 2023, please fill out the registration form. Registration will be open from Monday, March 15th through May 31st. For more details, please see the LunGradCon linktree.

Conference Format

Abstracts are due to lungradcon@gmail.com by midnight on May 31st.

Talks will be 8 minutes in length.

After each speaker is done, the session chairs will open the floor to questions from the audience about any of the topics in the session, and general discussion will be encouraged.

Each day will also have poster session, grouped by the different topics delineated above. Please follow the NESF guidelines for poster size/format. More information will be provided regarding the format and schedule for poster presentations.

Abstracts

Abstracts must be emailed to lungradcon@gmail.com until May 31st, 11:59pm PDT. Decisions on abstracts will be given in June.
