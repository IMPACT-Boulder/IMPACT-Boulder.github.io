  • Jamey Szalay, Univ. of Colorado at Boulder

    Jamey is a fourth-year graduate student at the Laboratory for Atmospheric and Space Physics and the Department of Physics at the University of Colorado at Boulder, working with Prof. Mihály Horányi. His research focuses on dusty space plasma physics throughout the solar system.

  • Parvathy Prem, The University of Texas at Austin

    Parvathy is a fourth-year graduate student in the Aerospace Engineering department at UT Austin, working with Prof. David Goldstein in the Computational Fluid Physics Lab. Her research focuses on using numerical simulations of lunar comet impacts to better understand how impact-delivered volatiles are transported on otherwise airless bodies, and to constrain how much water comets could have delivered to the Moon.

  • Dan Moriarty, Brown University

    Dan Moriarty is a fourth-year graduate student at Brown University working with Carle Pieters. His researches focuses on near-infrared spectroscopy of the Moon and other airless bodies in order to understand processes such as large impacts and space weathering.
